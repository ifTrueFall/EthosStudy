Zhuangzi Quiz

Q1. Zhuangzi is difficult to interpret for several reasons. However, one key aspect that we can take as central to his philosophical outlook is:

    A. Pleasure and the absence of pain
    B. Authenticity
    C. Virtue
    D. None of the options
    E. Universality
    F. Egoism

Q2. According to Zhuangzi, the philosophical focus on society and virtue:

    A. Misses the point of the particular and the local.
    B. None of the options.
    C. Is the best way to understand the particular and local.
    D. Is a significant departure from Confucian philosophy.
    E. Is exactly the right way to approach ethics.
    F. Is more important than the particular and local.

Q3. The concept of the Dao:

    A. Refers to the universal process or event.
    B. Is ineffable.
    C. Is central to Zhuangzi's understanding of self-cultivation.
    D. Is key to living an authentic life.
    E. None of the options.
    F. All of the options.

Q4. Central to Zhuangzi's conception of the dao, is understanding nature as:

    A. Fundamentally knowable.
    B. Substance.
    C. Created by God.
    D. None of the options.
    E. Imbued with meaning.
    F. A process.

Q5. "Fasting of the mind" refers to the practice of:

    A. Attempting to forget our past.
    B. Emptying the mind and thinking of nothing.
    C. Abstaining from food until one reaches mental clarity.
    D. Abstaining from the pursuit of education.
    E. Not applying concepts or values to our experiences.
    F. None of the options.
